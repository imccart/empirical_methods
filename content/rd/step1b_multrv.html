So far, our discussion of the RD design has focused on a single running variable and an exogenous assignment to treatment. However, in many empirical settings, a treatment assignment may be based on multiple underlying scores. Cattaneo, Titiunik, and Vazquez-Bare (2020) points to research where school admission is based on both reading and math scores. Another example is Keele and Titiunik (2015), which uses treatment assignment based on geographic location defined by latitude and longitude. There is a relatively new area of literature that proposes methodologies for this sort of analysis which may provide helpful solutions for your context (Cattaneo, Titiunik, and Vazquez-Bare 2020; Papay, Willett, and Murnane 2011; Reardon and Robinson 2012).
